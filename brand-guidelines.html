00

Brand psychology

Before pixels: the two playbooks this identity borrows from, and the rule each one gives us.

From Duolingo — be a friend, not a store

  • A mascot people root for. Folio is the brand's face, voice, and guilt trip. He appears wherever motivation is needed.
  • Habit loops beat campaigns. The reading streak is the product. Green calendar squares are the most persuasive design element we own.
  • Tactile UI. Chunky buttons that physically press down. Interfaces should feel like toys that respect you.
  • Cheerful menace. The voice teases, never shames. "Folio noticed you didn't read yesterday. Folio remembers."

From Apple — restraint is the luxury

  • One idea per screen. Every page has a single hero message set huge. Everything else steps back.
  • White is a color. Generous space around everything. If a layout feels empty, it's probably right.
  • One accent, disciplined. Green means action and progress. Yellow marks the special. Nothing else shouts.
  • Words like a human. Short, concrete, benefit-first. "Read daily. Get rewarded." — never "Unlock synergies."

Folio — a green dog with one folded ear — is both mascot and mark. The folded ear is the old dog-ear device made alive, always lined in sunny yellow. The wordmark sets “dogear” lowercase in Fredoka, friendly and unbreakable.

Components

Four signature parts, used identically on web, mobile, and social. If a component isn't on this list, it should look quiet.

Chunky press buttons

Solid fill, 5px darker bottom shadow, and they physically depress on click. Try it. Green = primary, yellow = celebration, ghost = secondary.

The fold

The sunny folded corner survives from v1 — it still means "worth marking." One fold per view, on featured cards only.

06

Motion, responsiveness & access

The quality floor. None of this is optional, on any surface.

Motion

Buttons press (100ms), cards lift (160ms), nothing else moves without purpose. All motion respects prefers-reduced-motion.

Responsive

Fluid type via clamp(). Grids: 4 → 3 → 2 columns; heroes stack under 900px; buttons go full-width under 440px. No horizontal scroll, ever.

Contrast

Body text is ink on white (15:1). White on Leaf is reserved for large bold text; small text on green uses Forest or moves to a white panel.

Focus

Every interactive element shows a 3px sunny outline on keyboard focus. The fun is optional; the keyboard is not.
